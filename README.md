# Store Sales Dashboard – Data Visualization & Storytelling
Tool used: Microsoft Power BI Desktop

## Objective
Build visualizations that go beyond just charts uncover and communicate business insights from sales data through an interactive dashboard and a storyboard summary.

## Dataset
store sales dataset — contains order-level sales, profit, quantity, category, sub-category, region, segment, and date fields.

## What I Did
- Built an interactive Power BI dashboard ("Dashboard1") with filters for Product Name, City, Month, and Year
- Created KPI cards for Total Quantity, Total Profit, and Total Sales
- Visualized sales and profit across Sub-Category, Segment, Category, Region, Quarter, and Year using a mix of bar, donut, pie, waterfall, and combo charts
- Added a second page ("Storyboard") summarizing the three key business insights in plain language, to satisfy the "storytelling" requirement rather than just presenting raw visuals

## Key Insights

**1. Profit leak in sub-categories**
Despite solid sales (₹207K), Tables post a -₹18K loss, and Supplies (₹47K sales) post a -₹1K loss. Phones drive both highest sales and highest profit (₹330K sales, ₹44K profit), making it the strongest performer in the store.

**2. Segment & category concentration**
The Consumer segment drives over half of total sales (50.57%, ₹1.16M) more than Corporate and Home Office combined. Furniture and Technology together account for ~68% of category sales, with Office Supplies the smallest contributor.

**3. Regional and yearly growth trend**
West (₹725.5K) and East (₹680.5K) are the top-performing regions, together contributing over 60% of sales, while South lags at ₹391.7K. Sales have grown steadily each year, from ₹484K in 2021 to ₹734K in 2024.



## Outcome
Practiced visual storytelling by pairing well-chosen chart types (avoiding chart clutter) with a dedicated insights page that translates raw numbers into business takeaways specifically identifying where the store is losing money despite strong sales volume.
